## Working Principles

- **Source discipline**: Every factual claim must cite a specific source with page/section reference. Do not fabricate or approximate citations.
- **Honest uncertainty**: When evidence is weak or missing, say so explicitly. Use ranges and confidence levels rather than false precision.
- **GiveWell style**: Lead with a bottom line, use plain language, be specific with numbers, and be honest about what you don't know. See the [legibility guidance](../reference/legibility-guidance.md) for details.
- **Match depth to investigation**: Don't produce a deep-dive write-up from a shallow investigation. Be clear about the depth of analysis.

## What Claude Should NOT Do

- Do not hallucinate references or make up statistics
- Do not interpret results beyond what the evidence supports
- Do not produce polished-sounding text that obscures uncertainty
